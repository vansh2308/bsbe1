Protein Structure Alignment
In this section we are going to use MM align Algorithm for protein structure alignment.
So first go to https://www.rcsb.org/to take the data about the proteins structures that you want to align.
Now on the search bar highlighted above, search the two specific type of proteins that you want to compare. Here for example we are going to compare
1. hemoglobin subunit alpha (PDB ID 4HHB, chain A) and
2. neuroglobin (PDB ID 1OJ6, chain D).
The information page will look something like this,
Now click on the download files button and download it in the format of PDBx/mmCIF format. Do this for other protein structure also. After downloading the file, go to https://zhanggroup.org/MM-align/ to perform the alignment.
Scroll down and upload the files in the space provided.
After uploading click on Run mm-align to see the results.
The example structures that we took had the result shown below.
Note the highlighted information in the above picture as it is very vital for us in understanding how good is the alignment. RMSD (root mean square deviation) is computed between aligned pairs of the backbone C-alpha atoms in superposed structures. The lower the RMSD, the better the structure alignment between the pair of structures. TM-score (template modeling score) is a measure of topological similarity between the template and model structures. The TM-score ranges between 0 and 1, where 1 indicates a perfect match and 0 is no match between the two structures. Scores < 0.2 usually indicate that the proteins are unrelated while those >0.5 generally have the same protein fold (e.g., classified by SCOP/CATH). Also, in the results two TM scores are given with taking each structure as reference. So after observing the results we found that RMSD value is 6.47 which is a high value and both the TM scores are also < 0.5. Thus this indicates the above alignment is not good.
